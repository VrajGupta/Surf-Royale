## Question

The visual style target for the world: grounded-photoreal vs. stylized-realistic, mood (sun-bleached vs. storm-grey), reference films/games/photography, and readability constraints art must obey (players must read swell, zone edge, and kelp/reef danger at a glance). Output: a direction statement + reference list, not concept art.

## Decision (2026-07-17)

Stylized-realistic (physics-readable water, pushed saturation, Uncharted-ocean school). Mood is dynamic: sun-bleached open → storm-grey endgame, synced to zone/weather (#10/#12) so mood doubles as the match clock. Reference spine is game-first: Sea of Thieves wave silhouettes, Uncharted 4 water lighting, Riders Republic energy. Four contractual readability rules: swell silhouettes at 300m+, reserved zone-edge hue, distinct hazard palette + texture break, and water clarity never below gameplay-legible in any weather.
